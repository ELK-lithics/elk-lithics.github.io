About Me

My name is Emmanuel La O Kirchner. I am a researcher specialising in lithic analysis, with a particular focus on the Neolithic of Britain. I am currently studying at the University of Halle-Wittenberg, Germany.

Since 2020, I have been investigating the Neolithic archaeology of East Hampshire, undertaking fieldwalking surveys, visiting museum-held collections, doing GIS-mapping and much more.

I am currently in the process of writing my bachelorâ€™s thesis at the University of Halle-Wittenberg, where I have been a student since October 2022. My thesis focuses on a flint scatter, which I uncovered through fieldwalking, dating primarily to the Late Neolithic. Amongst the assemblage is a high proportion of flint and chert mining picks, suggesting local flint mining activity.

Through my research, I argue that these picks, along with other mining-related evidence, can serve as a proxy for flint mining in the region.
