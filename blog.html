June 7th, 2024

After becoming an official volunteer with the Saxony-Anhalt State Department of Archaeology, I went on a fieldwalk around the city of Naumburg in Central Germany.

Although I was searching for Mesolithic and Upper Palaeolithic material, this beautiful Neolithic arrowhead caught my eye. This leaf-shaped style, although common in the UK and typically associated with the Early Neolithic, is a very rare form in Germany and likely dates to the Middle Neolithic.

March 21st, 2024

Today, I visited Guildford Museum to examine some finds discovered by various hobby collectors and antiquarians within my research area.

Among the collections was this group of unprovenanced Barbed and Tanged arrowheads, collected by hobbyist Henry Albert Mangles in the 1890s.
